# Go-To-Market Master Plan: Clinical KOL Intelligence Product

## 1. Executive Positioning
The technical infrastructure (PubMed extraction engine, SQL aggregation layer, and dynamic ingestion pipeline) is production-capable. The strategic focus now shifts to commercialization. The objective is rapid monetization through a tightly defined, high-value deliverable without expanding into dashboards, APIs, or enterprise integrations at this stage.

## 2. Product Definition (Version 1)
- Top 50–100 ranked investigators in a defined indication.
- 5-year publication count analysis.
- 24-month Scientific Velocity Score (publication acceleration).
- Corresponding author leadership signal.
- Institutional affiliation and geographic filtering.
- 1-page executive interpretation summary.
- 48–72 hour delivery SLA.

## 3. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- US-based oncology biotech (10–150 employees).
- IND filed or Phase I/II pipeline assets.
- Recently raised Series A/B funding.
- Actively hiring MSL or Clinical Operations roles.
- Regional CROs (<300 employees) responding to oncology RFPs.

## 4. Revenue Model & Validation Target
**Primary Entry Product:** 72-Hour Clinical KOL Intelligence Brief. 
**Recommended Price:** $2,450 per brief (below procurement friction threshold). 
**Validation Target:** 3–6 sales within first 30 days of outbound campaign. 
**Initial Revenue Validation Goal:** $10,000–$15,000.

## 5. Product Naming Strategy
**Primary Name:** Clinical KOL Intelligence Brief (CKIB). 
**Alternative Premium Brand Option:** Scientific Influence Velocity Report (SIVR). 
The primary name aligns with industry-standard terminology used in Medical Affairs and Clinical Development. The premium option emphasizes differentiation through velocity-based ranking.

## 6. Complete Product Description
The Clinical KOL Intelligence Brief is a rapid-response scientific intelligence deliverable designed for early-stage biotech and regional CRO teams requiring disease-specific investigator identification. Built on structured PubMed metadata and hierarchical MeSH disease classification, the report ranks investigators using a transparent Scientific Velocity Scoring framework that prioritizes recent publication acceleration over legacy citation metrics. The deliverable is optimized for advisory board formation, site feasibility planning, investigator shortlisting, and strategic scientific engagement.

## 7. Brand Deployment Strategy
- **Phase 1 (0–6 months):** Launch under MadeByDoc as a boutique clinical intelligence consultancy.
- Position as rapid scientific intelligence, not software.
- Focus on closing 5–10 paying customers before platform branding.
- **Phase 2 (post-validation):** Transition under MedDash.ai as Clinical Intelligence Division.
- Introduce subscription and dashboard after revenue validation.

## 8. LinkedIn Market Entry Strategy
- Avoid announcing as an 'AI platform launch'.
- Lead with pain-point positioning (site alignment, stale KOL data).
- Offer 3-profile sample teaser, not full free report.
- Use insight-driven posts showing velocity gaps in niche indications.
- Call-to-action: 'Message for a 3-profile sample.'

## 9. Phase 5: The 30-Day Pilot Execution Plan
This is the manual sprint designed to secure 3-6 paid pilots ($2,450/ea) using the BioCrawler -> Meddash tandem before full automation is deployed.

### The Math (Conversion Funnel)
To secure 3 closed-won pilots, we assume a highly conservative B2B conversion rate:
*   **Goal:** 3 Paid Pilots
*   **Target Teaser Acceptance Rate:** 20% (require 15 accepted Teasers)
*   **Target Outreach Volume:** 150 highly qualified CMOs/VPs.

### Weekly Sprint KPIs (Solo Founder Capacity)
This workflow is designed to be executed manually in 1-2 hours per day.

**Week 1: The BioCrawler Engine (List Building)**
*   Run the Lenovo BioCrawler to aggregate 200 "Tier A" US/CAN targets (funded/hiring).
*   Manually cross-reference these 200 companies on LinkedIn to find their CMO or VP of Clinical Operations.

**Weeks 2-3: The Manual Meddash Grind (The Wedge)**
*   **Daily Action:** Take 10 Tier A targets per day.
*   **Daily Action:** Run the Meddash SVS Pipeline for those 10 specific indications to find "Rising Stars".
*   **Daily Action:** Manually populate and send 10 "Feasibility Rescue" outreach templates via LinkedIn/Cold Email.
*   **KPI:** 50 highly personalized messages sent per week (150 total over 3 weeks).

**Week 4: The Handoff (Sales & Fulfillment)**
*   **Goal:** Secure 15 responses requesting the 3-profile Teaser.
*   **Fulfillment:** Generate the teaser PDFs (using Meddash data) and send them.
*   **The Ask:** Schedule a 15-minute alignment call to review the Teaser, pivoting to the sale of the full $2,450 72-Hour Intelligence Brief.

## 10. Phase 2: Enterprise SaaS Evolution
Once the manual 30-Day Sprint validates the data product with 3-5 paying pilot customers, Meddash will pivot from a consultancy model to a modular **Strategic Medical Intelligence Platform**.

### Product Architecture (Modular)
*   **Module 1 (KOL Intelligence):** SVS Rankings, Investigator Networks, emerging "Rising Stars". (Target: Medical Affairs / MSL Teams).
*   **Module 2 (Biotech Signals):** Funding signals, Phase 1/2 trial momentum, MSL hiring velocity. (Target: Strategy / BD Teams).
*   **Module 3 (Safety Signals):** FAERS integration and adverse event trends. (Target: Pharmacovigilance).

### Enterprise Licensing Model
Meddash will *not* deploy a per-user retail SaaS model. To protect the dataset from internal scraping and sharing, monetization will occur via **Enterprise Therapeutic Area Licensing**:

*   **Model A (Enterprise License):** $30,000 - $80,000 annually. Provides unlimited seats within a company, but data access is strictly bounded to a specific Therapeutic Area (e.g., Oncology-only).
*   **Model B (Hybrid Entry):** $2,000/month for a 10-seat "Team" license.
*   **Protection Mechanisms:** Export limits (max 200 rows) and strict legal "Internal Use Only" distribution clauses.

## 11. Execution Discipline
Ignore Phase 2 for now. Do not expand into dashboards, APIs, enterprise integrations, Bridge Engines, or complex software development before securing at least three paying customers. Focus exclusively on executing the 30-day manual sprint (Section 9) and delivering high-quality 72-hour briefs.
