## Findings
- **FTC CAN-SPAM (US):** The law makes no exception for B2B email. However, it's permissible if compliance steps are followed: (1) Don't use false or misleading header information, (2) Don't use deceptive subject lines, (3) Identify the message as an ad, (4) Tell recipients where you’re located, (5) Tell recipients how to opt out, (6) Honor opt-out requests promptly.
  - Screenshot: `ftc_can_spam_b2b_1775021798724.png`
- **ICO UK GDPR (UK/EU):** PECR rules for B2B electronic marketing do not apply to "corporate subscribers" (e.g., professional emails at hospitals). Consent is not required by PECR. Personal data processing (e.g., Name@hospital.com) requires a lawful basis under GDPR, with "Legitimate Interest" (Art 6.1.f) being the standard for B2B outreach. Transparency (Article 14) and Right to Object (Article 21) apply.
  - Screenshot: `ico_b2b_marketing_rules_1775021829358.png`
- **GDPR Article 14:** When data is not obtained from the data subject, information must be provided within a reasonable period (max 1 month). Exceptions include "disproportionate effort" and if it would "make the processing impossible or seriously impair the objective".
  - Screenshot: `gdpr_art14_checklist_1775021850000.png`
- **PhRMA/EFPIA Codes:** MSL activities are categorized as "non-promotional" scientific exchange. Peer-to-peer scientific dialogue with KOLs is permitted and encouraged as long as it's balanced, clinical, and not promotional (no sales involvement). Email is a standard channel for MSL communication.
  - Screenshot: `phrma_msl_scientific_exchange_1775021925344.png`
